African Spotted Creeper

Salpornis salvadori

The African spotted creeper is a small passerine bird, which is a member of the subfamily Salpornithinae of the treecreeper family Certhiidae. It is found in sub-Saharan Africa in open deciduous forest and mango groves. It does not migrate other than local movements

Behaviour & Diet

The African Spotted Creeper will spend a lot of its time crawling up the bark of tall trees. They will fly from one tree to the other. They fly from the top to the bottom of the other tree and then start climbing upwards. Where they would feed on small invertebre that are resting under the bark. They will use their decurved bill to dig under the bark and feel for insect.
