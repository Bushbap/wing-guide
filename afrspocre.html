African Spotted Creeper

Salpornis salvadori

The African spotted creeper is a small passerine bird, which is a member of the subfamily Salpornithinae of the treecreeper family Certhiidae. It is found in sub-Saharan Africa in open deciduous forest and mango groves. It does not migrate other than local movements

Description

a 15 cm dull brown bird dotted all over with white spots. They are indeed passerine having 3 toes forward and 1 toe backwards.They have a medium length bill.

Behaviour

The African Spotted Creeper will spend a lot of its time crawling up the bark of tall trees. They will fly from one tree to the other. They fly from the top to the bottom of the other tree and then start climbing upwards.

diet & call

They would use their long bill to probe underneath the bark looking to find any insect. at times they could also scare an insect and they would chase after it. They are passerine bird, naturally. They give a 2 - 3 noted soft whistle while perched.

Habitat & Distribution

It is found in sub-Saharan Africa in open deciduous forest and mango groves. It does not migrate other than local movements.
